## Jurisdictional Compliance Addendum  
**UK / EU / UAE**

This addendum supplements the standard KYC checklist and applies jurisdiction-specific regulatory and compliance requirements for physical commodity trading.

---

## 1. United Kingdom (UK)

### Regulatory Framework
KYC and AML obligations are governed primarily by the UK Money Laundering Regulations, OFSI sanctions rules, and FCA expectations where applicable.

### Required Checks
Confirmation of UK Companies House registration  
Verification of Persons with Significant Control (PSC register)  
Sanctions screening against UK OFSI Consolidated List  
PEP screening aligned with UK definitions  
Adverse media screening in English and relevant local languages  

### Documentation Expectations
Certificate of Incorporation (UK Companies House)  
PSC Register extract  
Proof of UK registered office  
Government-issued ID for directors and PSCs  

### Risk Considerations
Exposure to UK-sanctioned countries or entities  
Use of UK financial institutions or GBP clearing  
Trading activities involving dual-use or sensitive commodities  

---

## 2. European Union (EU)

### Regulatory Framework
Compliance is aligned with EU AML Directives (AMLD), EU Sanctions Regulations, and local competent authority guidance in the member state of incorporation.

### Required Checks
Verification via national business registries  
Identification of Ultimate Beneficial Owners under EU thresholds  
Sanctions screening against EU Consolidated Sanctions List  
PEP screening per EU AML definitions  
Assessment of cross-border intra-EU trade exposure  

### Documentation Expectations
Commercial register extract (e.g. Handelsregister, Registre du Commerce)  
UBO declaration compliant with EU standards  
Proof of registered address within the EU  
Identity documents for UBOs and directors  

### Risk Considerations
Transactions involving high-risk third countries under EU AML lists  
Complex ownership chains across multiple EU jurisdictions  
Use of EU ports subject to enhanced customs controls  

---

## 3. United Arab Emirates (UAE)

### Regulatory Framework
KYC obligations derive from UAE Federal AML Law, Cabinet Resolutions, and guidance from MOE, Central Bank, and relevant Free Zone authorities.

### Required Checks
Verification of trade license validity and issuing authority  
UBO declaration as per UAE requirements  
Sanctions screening against UAE Local Terrorist List and UN lists  
PEP screening including regional political exposure  
Assessment of Free Zone versus mainland entity status  

### Documentation Expectations
Trade License (Mainland or Free Zone)  
Certificate of Incorporation / Establishment Card  
UBO Declaration Form  
Passport copies for shareholders, directors, and signatories  

### Risk Considerations
Use of Free Zones with limited disclosure regimes  
Transit or re-export trading activity  
Exposure to sanctioned jurisdictions via indirect routing  

---

## 4. Enhanced Due Diligence Triggers

Enhanced Due Diligence should be applied where:
The counterparty operates in or trades with high-risk jurisdictions  
Ownership structures are opaque or layered  
Transactions involve sanctioned commodities or sensitive routes  
Payments are routed through non-standard banking channels
